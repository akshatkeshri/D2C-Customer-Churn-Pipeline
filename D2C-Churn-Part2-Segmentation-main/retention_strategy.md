# Customer Retention & Segmentation Strategy

## Executive Summary
This strategy document outlines the retention actions for the D2C customer base (2,400 total users) utilizing Recency, Frequency, and Monetary (RFM) modeling enriched with web session and support ticket signals. Rather than applying a universal discount strategy, this approach targets specific cohorts to maximize Return on Investment (ROI).

## Segment Breakdowns & Recommended Actions

### 1. High-Value but Unhappy (45 Customers)
* **Behavior:** These are top-quartile spenders who have submitted 2 or more support tickets recently. 
* **Action:** Direct human-in-the-loop intervention. Route to a senior customer success representative for a personalized check-in and VIP resolution.
* **Business Value:** Extremely high. Preventing the churn of these 45 users protects a disproportionate amount of top-line revenue.

### 2. At-Risk Customers (324 Customers)
* **Behavior:** High historical frequency and monetary value, but their recency has slipped significantly. They are fading away.
* **Action:** Aggressive "Win-Back" campaigns. Utilize personalized emails highlighting new products in their preferred categories, coupled with a high-value, time-sensitive discount code (e.g., 20% off next purchase).
* **Business Value:** High. It is cheaper to re-engage these proven buyers than to acquire net-new customers of the same lifetime value.

### 3. Discount-Sensitive (513 Customers)
* **Behavior:** Lower purchase frequency, but actively abandoning carts. They are browsing and showing intent but waiting for a price drop.
* **Action:** Automated trigger emails containing 10% to 15% discount codes sent 24 hours after a cart is abandoned.
* **Business Value:** Medium. Converts immediate browsing intent into captured revenue, clearing stagnant inventory.

### 4. Champions (593 Customers)
* **Behavior:** Recent, frequent, and high-spending buyers.
* **Action:** Do NOT offer discounts—this would unnecessarily erode profit margins. Instead, offer early access to new product lines and invite them to a referral program.
* **Business Value:** High. Maximizes organic margin and leverages them for low-cost customer acquisition.

### 5. Loyal Customers (497 Customers)
* **Behavior:** Steady, average metrics across the board. The stable core of the business.
* **Action:** Enroll in a standard points-based loyalty tier to encourage incremental frequency increases.

### 6. Dormant Customers (428 Customers)
* **Behavior:** Low spend, low frequency, and highly un-recent. 
* **Action:** Exclude from paid ad retargeting to save budget. Send a final, automated "We miss you" email before removing them from the active CRM list to improve deliverability metrics.

## Campaign Budget Prioritization
**Recommendation:** Allocate the majority of the immediate retention budget to the **At-Risk Customers** (324 users) and the **High-Value but Unhappy** (45 users). 

These two groups represent proven, high-revenue individuals who are actively slipping away. Spending marketing dollars to save these 369 specific customers will yield a vastly higher ROI than blindly offering discounts to the 593 Champions (who will buy anyway) or the 428 Dormant customers (who are unlikely to return).
